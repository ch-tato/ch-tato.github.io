History of Madura

A vertical journey through the key moments that shaped the island of Madura.

900

Ancient Origins

Early history of Madura is intertwined with the major Javanese kingdoms like Singhasari and Majapahit. It was known as a source of salt and skilled sailors, often playing a strategic role in regional maritime power struggles.

1650

The Rise of Cakraningrat

The Cakraningrat dynasty rises as a powerful local force in West Madura. They become key allies of the Mataram Sultanate and, later, the Dutch East India Company (VOC), significantly influencing the politics of Java and Madura.

1750

Dutch Colonial Era

By the mid-18th century, Madura falls completely under Dutch colonial control. The island is administratively divided, and its economy is focused on salt production for the colonial government. This period also sees the rise of distinct cultural practices.

1945

Indonesian Independence

Following Indonesia's declaration of independence, Madura is integrated into the Republic of Indonesia. It becomes part of the East Java province, maintaining its unique cultural identity, language, and traditions within the larger nation.

2009

The Suramadu Bridge

The opening of the Suramadu Bridge, the longest in Indonesia, marks a new era for Madura. It dramatically improves connectivity with Java, boosting economic development, tourism, and accessibility to the island.

Iconic Madurese Cuisine

A taste of the three most popular and beloved dishes from the island.

Sate Madura

Arguably the most famous dish, this chicken or mutton satay is characterized by its small pieces of meat and a distinctive peanut sauce mixed with sweet soy sauce, garlic, and shallots, grilled over hot charcoal.

Bebek Sinjay

A legendary fried duck dish known for its crispy skin and tender, savory meat. It's typically served with steamed rice, fresh vegetables, and a unique, spicy mango sambal that perfectly complements the rich flavor of the duck.

Soto Madura

A clear, aromatic beef soup with a yellowish broth from turmeric. This hearty soto is filled with beef or offal, glass noodles, and boiled potato slices, often topped with fried shallots and served with a side of sambal.

Popular Tourist Destinations

Explore three of the most captivating and visited spots in Madura.

Bukit Jaddih

A former limestone quarry transformed into a stunning landscape of white chalk hills and turquoise water pools. Its unique, almost otherworldly appearance makes it a popular spot for photography and exploration.

Gili Labak

A tiny, pristine island off the southeast coast of Madura, famous for its white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters. It's a paradise for snorkeling, diving, and those seeking a tranquil beach escape.

Air Terjun Toroan

A unique waterfall located on the coast that flows directly into the sea. The combination of the freshwater falls and the saltwater of the Madura Strait creates a spectacular and rare natural phenomenon.
